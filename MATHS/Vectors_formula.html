Vectors - All Formulas

Complete reference guide for vector algebra including definitions, operations, properties, and important results for CBSE and other board exams

Basic Definitions

Vector: \(\vec{a} = a_1\hat{i} + a_2\hat{j} + a_3\hat{k}\)
Magnitude: \(|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{a_1^2 + a_2^2 + a_3^2}\)
Unit vector: \(\hat{a} = \frac{\vec{a}}{|\vec{a}|}\)

A vector has both magnitude and direction. The unit vectors along x, y, z axes are \(\hat{i}, \hat{j}, \hat{k}\) respectively.

Vector Operations

Addition: \(\vec{a} + \vec{b} = (a_1+b_1)\hat{i} + (a_2+b_2)\hat{j} + (a_3+b_3)\hat{k}\)
Subtraction: \(\vec{a} - \vec{b} = (a_1-b_1)\hat{i} + (a_2-b_2)\hat{j} + (a_3-b_3)\hat{k}\)
Scalar Multiplication: \(m\vec{a} = ma_1\hat{i} + ma_2\hat{j} + ma_3\hat{k}\)

Vector addition follows triangle law and parallelogram law. Scalar multiplication changes magnitude but not direction (unless scalar is negative).

Dot Product (Scalar Product)

\(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\cos\theta\)
\(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_1b_1 + a_2b_2 + a_3b_3\)
Angle between vectors: \(\cos\theta = \frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{a}||\vec{b}|}\)

Dot product gives a scalar quantity. Two vectors are perpendicular if \(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 0\).

Cross Product (Vector Product)

\(\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\sin\theta\ \hat{n}\)
\(\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \end{vmatrix}\)
Magnitude: \(|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}| = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\sin\theta\)

Cross product gives a vector perpendicular to both \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\). Two vectors are parallel if \(\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \vec{0}\).

Properties of Dot & Cross Product

Commutative: \(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = \vec{b} \cdot \vec{a}\)
Not Commutative: \(\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = -(\vec{b} \times \vec{a})\)
Distributive: \(\vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} + \vec{c}) = \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} + \vec{a} \cdot \vec{c}\)
Distributive: \(\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} + \vec{c}) = \vec{a} \times \vec{b} + \vec{a} \times \vec{c}\)

Dot product is commutative but cross product is anti-commutative. Both follow distributive law over addition.

Scalar Triple Product

\([\vec{a}\ \vec{b}\ \vec{c}] = \vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{c})\)
\([\vec{a}\ \vec{b}\ \vec{c}] = \begin{vmatrix} a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \\ c_1 & c_2 & c_3 \end{vmatrix}\)
Cyclic Property: \([\vec{a}\ \vec{b}\ \vec{c}] = [\vec{b}\ \vec{c}\ \vec{a}] = [\vec{c}\ \vec{a}\ \vec{b}]\)

Scalar triple product gives the volume of parallelepiped formed by vectors \(\vec{a}, \vec{b}, \vec{c}\). Vectors are coplanar if \([\vec{a}\ \vec{b}\ \vec{c}] = 0\).

Vector Triple Product

\(\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) = (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{c})\vec{b} - (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})\vec{c}\)
\((\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \times \vec{c} = (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{c})\vec{b} - (\vec{b} \cdot \vec{c})\vec{a}\)

Vector triple product results in a vector in the plane of \(\vec{b}\) and \(\vec{c}\). Note that \(\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \neq (\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \times \vec{c}\).

Projection of Vectors

Projection of \(\vec{a}\) on \(\vec{b}\): \(\frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{b}|}\)
Component of \(\vec{a}\) along \(\vec{b}\): \(\left(\frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{b}|^2}\right) \vec{b}\)
Projection of \(\vec{a}\) perpendicular to \(\vec{b}\): \(\vec{a} - \left(\frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{b}|^2}\right) \vec{b}\)

Projection gives the scalar length of shadow, while component gives the vector in direction of \(\vec{b}\).

Important Results & Identities

\(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{a} = |\vec{a}|^2\)
\(\vec{a} \times \vec{a} = \vec{0}\)
Lagrange's Identity: \(|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}|^2 = |\vec{a}|^2|\vec{b}|^2 - (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})^2\)
\(|\vec{a} + \vec{b}|^2 = |\vec{a}|^2 + |\vec{b}|^2 + 2(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})\)
\(|\vec{a} - \vec{b}|^2 = |\vec{a}|^2 + |\vec{b}|^2 - 2(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})\)

These identities are frequently used in solving vector algebra problems.

Important Notes

  • Vectors are denoted by bold letters (\(\mathbf{a}\)) or with an arrow (\(\vec{a}\)).
  • A zero vector (\(\vec{0}\)) has magnitude 0 and no specific direction.
  • Two vectors are equal if they have same magnitude and direction.
  • Dot product is also called scalar product and cross product is also called vector product.
  • Scalar triple product is zero if any two vectors are equal or all three are coplanar.
  • For position vectors, \(\overrightarrow{AB} = \vec{b} - \vec{a}\) where \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\) are position vectors of A and B.
