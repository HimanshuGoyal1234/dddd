Chemical Reactions and Equations

1. Introduction to Chemical Reactions

Chemical Reaction: A process where substances (reactants) transform into new substances (products) with different properties.
Key Indicators:
  • Change in state (gas evolved, precipitate formed)
  • Change in color
  • Change in temperature (exothermic/endothermic)
  • Evolution of gas
Example (Burning of Magnesium):
2Mg(s) + O2(g) → 2MgO(s)

Observation: Bright white flame, white ash formed (MgO).

2. Chemical Equations

2.1 Writing Chemical Equations

Representation using symbols and formulas:

Reactants → Products
Symbols Used:
  • (s) - Solid
  • (l) - Liquid
  • (g) - Gas
  • (aq) - Aqueous solution
  • Δ - Heat required

2.2 Balancing Chemical Equations

Law of Conservation of Mass: Atoms cannot be created or destroyed.

Unbalanced:
H2 + O2 → H2O
Balanced:
2H2 + O2 → 2H2O

Steps: Count atoms → Adjust coefficients → Verify balance.

3. Types of Chemical Reactions

Type Definition General Form Example
Combination Two or more reactants form one product A + B → AB 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O
Decomposition One compound breaks into simpler substances AB → A + B 2H2O → 2H2 + O2
Displacement More reactive element replaces less reactive one A + BC → AC + B Zn + CuSO4 → ZnSO4 + Cu
Double Displacement Ions exchange between two compounds AB + CD → AD + CB AgNO3 + NaCl → AgCl + NaNO3
Redox Oxidation + Reduction occur simultaneously Oxidation: Loss of electrons
Reduction: Gain of electrons		 CuO + H2 → Cu + H2O

4. Key Concepts

4.1 Oxidation and Reduction

Oxidation: Gain of oxygen/loss of electrons.
Reduction: Loss of oxygen/gain of electrons.
CuO + H2 → Cu + H2O

Analysis:
- CuO is reduced (loses oxygen)
- H2 is oxidized (gains oxygen)

4.2 Corrosion and Rancidity

Corrosion: Oxidation of metals (e.g., rusting of iron).
4Fe + 3O2 + xH2O → 2Fe2O3.xH2O
Preventing Corrosion: Galvanization, painting, alloying.
Rancidity: Oxidation of fats/oils causing bad smell.
Prevention: Antioxidants, nitrogen packaging, refrigeration.
