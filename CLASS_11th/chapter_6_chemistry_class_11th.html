Class 11 Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics is the branch of physics that deals with heat, work, and temperature, and their relation to energy, radiation, and physical properties of matter. This mobile-friendly guide covers all essential concepts for Class 11.

Basic Concepts

Thermodynamic System

A thermodynamic system is defined as a quantity of matter or a region in space chosen for study. The mass or region outside the system is called the surroundings.

  • Open System: Can exchange both energy and matter with surroundings
  • Closed System: Can exchange only energy with surroundings
  • Isolated System: Cannot exchange either energy or matter with surroundings

Thermodynamic Equilibrium

A system is in thermodynamic equilibrium when it is in mechanical, thermal, and chemical equilibrium simultaneously.

Key Thermodynamic Parameters

Parameter Symbol Definition SI Unit
Internal Energy U The total energy contained within a system Joule (J)
Enthalpy H Heat content at constant pressure (H = U + PV) Joule (J)
Entropy S Measure of disorder or randomness J/K
Gibbs Free Energy G Energy available to do useful work (G = H - TS) Joule (J)
Helmholtz Free Energy A Maximum work at constant temperature (A = U - TS) Joule (J)

Laws of Thermodynamics

Zeroth Law

If two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third system, they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.

If A ≡ B and B ≡ C, then A ≡ C

First Law

The law of conservation of energy applied to thermodynamic systems.

ΔU = Q - W

Where ΔU is change in internal energy, Q is heat added to system, and W is work done by system.

Second Law

Heat cannot spontaneously flow from a colder location to a hotter location.

ΔSuniverse ≥ 0

Where ΔS is the change in entropy of the universe.

Thermodynamic Processes

Isothermal Process

A process that occurs at constant temperature.

ΔT = 0 ⇒ ΔU = 0

Adiabatic Process

A process that occurs without transfer of heat or matter between a system and its surroundings.

Q = 0

Isothermal Reversible Expansion

Ideal Gas Expansion

For an ideal gas undergoing isothermal reversible expansion:

PV = nRT = constant (Boyle's Law)

The work done during isothermal reversible expansion:

W = -nRT ln(V₂/V₁)

Enthalpy

Enthalpy Concepts

Enthalpy (H) is a state function defined as:

H = U + PV

For a process at constant pressure:

ΔH = ΔU + PΔV = Qp

For ideal gases, enthalpy depends only on temperature:

ΔH = nCpΔT
