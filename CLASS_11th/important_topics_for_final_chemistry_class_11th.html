Dalton‚Äôs Atomic Model (1808)

Model:

All matter is made up of indivisible atoms.

Atoms of a given element are identical in mass and properties.

Compounds are formed by the combination of atoms in fixed ratios.

Limitation:

Dalton considered atoms to be indivisible, but later discovery of subatomic particles (electron, proton, neutron) proved that atoms are divisible.

Thomson‚Äôs Model (1897)

Model:

  • Atom is a positively charged sphere with negatively charged electrons embedded in it
  • Total positive charge = Total negative charge, so atom is electrically neutral.
  • Electrons were not stationary, but embedded in the positive mass like seeds in watermelon.
  • Known as "Watermelon Model" too üçâ
  • Known as "Plum Pudding Model" too

Limitation:

  • Atom ke nucleus ka koi mention nahi (nucleus ke baare mein kuch nhi bataya)
  • Electrons ka fix jagah bata diya, par motion explain nahi kiya

Bohr‚Äôs Atomic Model (1913)

Main Features:

  • Electrons revolve in fixed circular orbits (energy levels) around the nucleus.
  • Energy is absorbed or emitted when electrons jump between orbits.

Limitation:

  • Could not explain the spectra of multi-electron atoms.
  • Failed to explain the Zeeman effect (effect of magnetic field) and Stark effect (effect of electric field).

Quantum Mechanical Model (Modern Model)

Main Features:

  • Electrons are found in orbitals ‚Äî regions with high probability of finding electrons.
  • Based on Schr√∂dinger‚Äôs wave equation and Heisenberg‚Äôs Uncertainty Principle.
  • Describes electron beheavior in terms of quantum numbers.

Limitation:

  • No major limitations; this is the most accepted and accurate model to date.

Modern Periodic Law ‚Äì Definition:

The physical and chemical properties of elements are a periodic function of their atomic numbers.

Hindi:-

Jab hum elements ko unke atomic number ke order mein arrange karte hain (jaise Periodic Table mein), toh unke guno mein (properties) ek pattern ya repeat hona dikhayi deta hai. Yeh repeat hona hi "periodicity" kehlata hai.

Definition of Modern Periodic Table:

The modern periodic table is the arrangement of elements in the increasing order of their atomic numbers, such that elements with similar properties fall in the same vertical group.

Key Features of the Modern Periodic Table:

  • Total groups (columns) = 18
  • Total groups (rows) = 7

Divided into:

  • s-block
  • p-block
  • d-block (transition metals)
  • f-block (lanthanides and actinides)

Ionization Enthalpy:

It is the minimum energy required to remove the most loosely bound electron from an isolated gaseous atom in its ground state.

Electron Gain Enthalpy:

It is the energy change when an electron is added to an isolated gaseous atom to form a negative ion.

What is Diagonal Relationship?

The diagonal relationship is the similarity in properties between elements placed diagonally in the periodic table, particularly between second and third period elements.
Example: Li (group 1, period 2) is similar to Mg (group 2, period 3).
