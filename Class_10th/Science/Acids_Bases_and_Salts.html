Acids, Bases and Salts

Class 10 Science | Chapter 2 | NCERT

1. Introduction to Acids and Bases

Acid: Substance that releases H⁺ ions in aqueous solution (pH < 7).
Base: Substance that releases OH⁻ ions in aqueous solution (pH > 7).

1.1 Properties

Property Acids Bases
Taste Sour (e.g., lemon) Bitter (e.g., soap)
Touch - Soapy/slippery
Reaction with metals Produce H₂ gas No reaction
Conductivity Good conductors Good conductors

Natural Indicators: Turmeric, red cabbage, beetroot.

3. pH Scale (0-14)

  • pH < 7: Acidic (Lower = Stronger acid)
  • pH = 7: Neutral (Water)
  • pH > 7: Basic/Alkaline (Higher = Stronger base)

4. Important Reactions

4.1 Acid + Metal

2HCl + Zn → ZnCl₂ + H₂↑

Observation: Bubbles of hydrogen gas.

4.2 Acid + Base (Neutralization)

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

Application: Antacids (like Mg(OH)₂) for acidity relief.

4.3 Acid + Carbonate/Bicarbonate

2HCl + Na₂CO₃ → 2NaCl + H₂O + CO₂↑

5. Salts

Salt: Ionic compound formed by neutralization (acid + base).
Type Example Uses
Sodium Chloride (NaCl) Common salt Food preservative, seasoning
Washing Soda (Na₂CO₃.10H₂O) Sodium carbonate Cleaning, glass manufacturing
Baking Soda (NaHCO₃) Sodium bicarbonate Antacid, fire extinguisher
Bleaching Powder (CaOCl₂) Calcium oxychloride Disinfectant, bleaching

6. Everyday Applications

  • Tooth Decay: pH < 5.5 dissolves enamel (acid from bacteria).
  • Soil Treatment: Lime (CaO) added to acidic soil.
  • Ant Sting: Formic acid neutralized with baking soda.

7. Practice Questions

  1. What is the pH range of human blood?
  2. Write the reaction between sulfuric acid and aluminum hydroxide.
  3. Why does turmeric stain turn red with soap?
Answers:
1. 7.35-7.45 (slightly basic)
2. 3H₂SO₄ + 2Al(OH)₃ → Al₂(SO₄)₃ + 6H₂O
3. Soap is basic, turmeric is a natural indicator (yellow → red in base).
