1. Concept of Disability and Disorder

Types of Disabilities

Physical Disabilities

  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Spinal Cord Injuries
  • Amputation
  • Muscular Dystrophy

Intellectual Disabilities

  • Down Syndrome
  • Autism Spectrum
  • Learning Disabilities
  • ADHD

Difference Between Disability and Disorder

Aspect Disability Disorder
Definition Physical/mental condition limiting activities Disruption of normal functions
Nature Often permanent May be temporary or permanent
Examples Blindness, paralysis Anxiety disorder, bipolar disorder

3. Advantages of Physical Activities for CWSN

Physical Benefits

  • Improves motor skills and coordination
  • Enhances cardiovascular health
  • Increases muscle strength and flexibility
  • Helps manage weight

Psychological Benefits

Emotional Benefits

  • Boosts self-esteem
  • Reduces depression
  • Improves mood

Social Benefits

  • Enhances social interaction
  • Develops teamwork skills
  • Reduces social isolation

4. Adaptive Physical Education

Principles of Adaptive PE

  1. Individualization: Tailor activities to each student's abilities
  2. Inclusion: Integrate with non-disabled peers when possible
  3. Modification: Adapt rules, equipment and environment
  4. Safety First: Ensure all activities are safe
  5. Progression: Gradually increase difficulty

Adaptive Sports Equipment

Wheelchair Basketball

Specially designed wheelchairs with anti-tip bars

Goalball

Eyeshades and bells inside ball for visually impaired

Boccia

Leather balls and ramps for severe physical disabilities
