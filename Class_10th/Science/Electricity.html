Electricity - Class 10 Physics

1. Electric Current

Electric current is the flow of electric charge. The SI unit of current is Ampere (A).

I = Q/t
Where:
I = Current (Ampere)
Q = Charge (Coulomb)
t = Time (seconds)

2. Potential Difference

The work done to move a unit charge from one point to another is called potential difference.

V = W/Q
Where:
V = Potential difference (Volt)
W = Work done (Joule)
Q = Charge (Coulomb)

3. Ohm's Law

The current through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference across its ends.

V = I × R
Where:
V = Potential difference (Volt)
I = Current (Ampere)
R = Resistance (Ohm)

Example:

A current of 0.5 A flows through a conductor when a potential difference of 2 V is applied. What is the resistance?

Solution: R = V/I = 2/0.5 = 4 Ω

4. Resistance

Resistance depends on:

  • Material of conductor
  • Length (l) - R ∝ l
  • Area of cross-section (A) - R ∝ 1/A
  • Temperature
R = ρ × l/A
Where:
ρ = Resistivity (Ωm)
l = Length (m)
A = Area (m²)

5. Series and Parallel Circuits

Feature Series Parallel
Current Same through all components Divides among branches
Voltage Divides among components Same across all branches
Total Resistance Rtotal = R1 + R2 + ... 1/Rtotal = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ...

6. Electric Power

P = V × I
P = I² × R
P = V²/R
Where:
P = Power (Watt)
V = Voltage (Volt)
I = Current (Ampere)
R = Resistance (Ohm)

Example:

An electric bulb is rated 220V and 100W. What is its resistance?

Solution: R = V²/P = (220)²/100 = 48400/100 = 484 Ω

7. Commercial Unit of Energy

1 kilowatt-hour (kWh) is the energy consumed when 1 kW power is used for 1 hour.

1 kWh = 3.6 × 106 J
