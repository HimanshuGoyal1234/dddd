Heart size, stroke volume, cardiac output

Respiratory System

Lung capacity, VO₂ max, breathing efficiency

Reaction time, coordination, motor unit recruitment

System Short-term Effects Long-term Adaptations

Respiratory Increased breathing rate, tidal volume Improved lung capacity, VO₂ max

3. Injury Prevention and Management

Prevention Strategies

Proper Warm-up

Dynamic stretching, sport-specific movements

Equipment

Appropriate footwear, protective gear

Technique

Proper form and biomechanics

Recovery

Adequate rest, sleep, nutrition

PRICE Principle for Acute Injuries

  1. Protection: Prevent further injury
  2. Rest: Avoid aggravating movements
  3. Ice: Apply for 15-20 mins every 2-3 hours
  4. Compression: Use elastic bandage
  5. Elevation: Raise above heart level

Apply immediately after injury for 24-48 hours

4. Sports First Aid

Essential First Aid for Common Injuries

Injury First Aid Do Not
Sprain/Strain PRICE, pain relief Apply heat initially, massage
Fracture Immobilize, ice, seek medical help Attempt to realign bones
Concussion Remove from play, monitor symptoms Return to play same day

First Aid Kit Essentials

  • Sterile gauze pads and bandages
  • Adhesive tape and elastic bandages
  • Antiseptic wipes and ointment
  • Ice packs and disposable gloves
  • Scissors and tweezers
  • Pain relievers (NSAIDs)

5. Rehabilitation Process

Phases of Sports Injury Rehabilitation

  1. Acute Phase (1-3 days): Control swelling/pain (PRICE)
  2. Subacute Phase (3 days-3 weeks): Restore range of motion
  3. Remodeling Phase (3-6 weeks): Strength and flexibility
  4. Functional Phase (6+ weeks): Sport-specific drills
  5. Return to Play: Gradual reintroduction to sport

Common Rehabilitation Exercises

Knee Injuries

  • Straight leg raises
  • Mini squats
  • Step-ups
  • Balance exercises

Shoulder Injuries

  • Pendulum exercises
  • Wall push-ups
  • Resistance band work
  • Rotator cuff strengthening
