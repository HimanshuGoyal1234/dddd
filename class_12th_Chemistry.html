Chapter 1: Solutions

1. Basic Concepts & Types of Solutions
Definition: Solution ek homogenous mixture hai jisme ek chhoti amount ki cheez (Solute) badi amount ki cheez (Solvent) mein ghul jaati hai.

Types based on Solvent state:

  • Gaseous Solution: Gas in Gas (Air), Gas in Liquid (Oxygen in water).
  • Liquid Solution: Liquid in Liquid (Alcohol in water), Solid in Liquid (Sugar in water).
  • Solid Solution: Solid in Solid (Alloys like Brass).
Note: Alloy (e.g., Brass, Bronze) ek solid solution mana jata hai.
2. Concentration Terms (Formulas)
Molarity (M) = Moles of Solute / Volume of Solution (in L)
Molality (m) = Moles of Solute / Mass of Solvent (in kg)
Mole Fraction (X) = Moles of Component / Total Moles

Important Relation (Temperature Dependence):

  • Molarity: Temperature badhne se change hoti hai kyunki volume expand karta hai. (Isliye ye temperature dependent hai).
  • Molality: Temperature badhne pe change nahi hoti kyunki mass constant rehta hai. (Temperature independent).
3. Raoult's Law & Vapour Pressure
Raoult's Law (For Volatile Liquids): Solution ka total vapour pressure, har component ke partial vapour pressure ka sum hota hai.
P_total = P_A¬∞ + P_B¬∞

Ideal vs Non-Ideal Solutions:

  • Ideal: Raoult's law follow karte hain. ŒîH_mix = 0, ŒîV_mix = 0. Example: Benzene + Toluene.
  • Non-Ideal (Positive Deviation): P_observed > P_calculated. ŒîH > 0 (Endothermic). Example: Acetone + Ethanol.
  • Non-Ideal (Negative Deviation): P_observed < P_calculated. ŒîH < 0 (Exothermic). Example: Chloroform + Acetone.

Azeotropes:

Ye aise mixtures hain jo distillation ke dauran same composition ke sath boil karte hain.

  • Minimum Boiling Azeotrope: Positive deviation (95% Ethanol + Water).
  • Maximum Boiling Azeotrope: Negative deviation (68% Nitric Acid + Water).
4. Colligative Properties (The Heart of Chapter)

Ye properties sirf solute ke particles ki number par depend karti hain, unki nature par nahi.

(a) Relative Lowering of Vapour Pressure:

ŒîP / P¬∞ = X_solute (Mole fraction of solute)

(b) Elevation in Boiling Point (ŒîT_b):

Impurity dalne se liquid ko boil karne ke liye zyada heat chahiye.

ŒîT_b = K_b * m (K_b = Molal elevation constant)

(c) Depression in Freezing Point (ŒîT_f):

Impurity dalne se liquid jaldi freeze hota hai.

ŒîT_f = K_f * m (K_f = Molal depression constant)

(d) Osmotic Pressure (œÄ):

Semipermeable membrane ke through solvent ka flow. Ye sabse best method hai molar mass nikalne ke liye kyunki isse temperature ka effect kam padta hai.

œÄ = CRT (C = Molarity, R = Gas const, T = Temp)
5. Abnormal Molar Mass & Van't Hoff Factor (i)

Kabhi-kabhi electrolytes (jaise NaCl) water mein toot ke ions ban jaate hain, toh particles ka number badh jata hai. Isliye molar mass abnormal milta hai.

i = Normal Molar Mass / Observed Molar Mass
i = 1 + (n-1)Œ± (n = ions count, Œ± = dissociation degree)
Yaad Rakho:
ŒîT_b (observed) = i * K_b * m
ŒîT_f (observed) = i * K_f * m
Osmotic Pressure (œÄ) = i * CRT

Chapter 3: Chemical Kinetics

1. Rate of Reaction
Rate = -d[R]/dt = +d[P]/dt

Instantaneous rate batata hai ki us particular pal par reaction kitni tezi se ho raha hai.

2. Order vs Molecularity
Order Molecularity
Experiment ke nikaalte hain. Theoretical concept hai.
Zero, fractional ho sakta hai. Hamesha whole number (1, 2, 3) hota hai.
Complex reactions ke liye apply hota hai. Sirf elementary steps ke liye.
3. Integrated Rate Laws (Sabse Important)

Zero Order Reaction:

Rate independent hai concentration se.

k = [R]_0 - [R]_t / t | t¬Ω = [R]_0 / 2k

First Order Reaction:

Rate directly proportional hai concentration ke square ke.

k = (2.303/t) * log ([R]_0/[R]_t) | t¬Ω = 0.693 / k
Graphs: Zero order: [R] vs Time is straight line (negative slope). First order: log[R] vs Time is straight line.
4. Arrhenius Equation & Temperature

Temperature badhne se rate badhti hai kyunki zyada molecules activation energy (Ea) ko cross kar lete hain.

k = A e^(-Ea/RT)
log k = log A - (Ea/2.303R) * (1/T)

Catalyst: Ea ko kam karta hai, taaki zyada molecules react kar sakein. Rate constant (k) badh jata hai.

  • Ligand: Electron donor jo metal se bind hota hai. (Example: NH‚ÇÉ, H‚ÇÇO, CN‚Åª, CO).

Chapter 6: Haloalkanes and Haloarenes

1. Classification & Nomenclature

Alkyl halides ke 3 types:

2. SN1 vs SN2 Mechanisms (Crucial)

SN2 (Substitution Nucleophilic Bimolecular):

  • One step mechanism.
  • Rate = k [RX][Nu‚Åª].
  • Backside attack, Inversion of configuration (Walden Inversion).
  • Order: Methyl > 1¬∞ > 2¬∞ >>> 3¬∞ (3¬∞ mein nahi hota).

Chapter 8: Aldehydes, Ketones & Carboxylic Acids

1. Aldehydes vs Ketones (Structure & Reactivity)

Dono mein carbonyl group (>C=O) hota hai.

  • Aldehydes (R-CHO): Terminal H atom hota hai. Zyada reactive. Easily oxidized (Reducing agents).
  • Ketones (R-CO-R): No terminal H. Kam reactive. Difficult to oxidize.
2. Nucleophilic Addition Reaction

C=O bond polar hota hai. Carbon +ve (electrophile), Oxygen -ve. Nucleophile (+ve charge wala) Carbon par attack karta hai.

Important Reagents:

  • HCN ‚Üí Cyanohydrin formation.
  • NaHSO‚ÇÉ ‚Üí Bisulphite adduct (crystalline solid).
  • Grignard Reagent ‚Üí Secondary/Tertiary alcohols.
  • Derivatives: 2,4-DNP (Orange crystals), Oxime, Semicarbazone, Hydrazone.
