Inverse Trigonometric Functions and Identities

Introduction to Trigonometry

Trigonometry (from Greek trigonon "triangle" + metron "measure") is the study of relationships between angles and sides of triangles, particularly right triangles.

Right Triangle Configuration:

  • Right angle (90°) at vertex B
  • Angle θ at vertex A
  • Third angle at vertex C (90° - θ)

Trigonometric Identities

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
sin(A±B) = sinAcosB ± cosAsinB
cos(A±B) = cosAcosB ∓ sinAsinB
tan(A±B) = (tanA±tanB)/(1∓tanAtanB)
sin2A = 2sinAcosA
cos2A = cos²A - sin²A
tan2A = 2tanA/(1-tan²A)
sinA + sinB = 2sin[(A+B)/2]cos[(A-B)/2]
cosA + cosB = 2cos[(A+B)/2]cos[(A-B)/2]

Inverse Trigonometric Functions

sin⁻¹x + cos⁻¹x = π/2
tan⁻¹x + cot⁻¹x = π/2
tan⁻¹x + tan⁻¹y = tan⁻¹[(x+y)/(1-xy)]
2tan⁻¹x = sin⁻¹[2x/(1+x²)]
sin⁻¹x = tan⁻¹[x/√(1-x²)]
cos⁻¹x = tan⁻¹[√(1-x²)/x]

General Solutions

sinθ = sinα ⇒ θ = nπ + (-1)ⁿα
cosθ = cosα ⇒ θ = 2nπ ± α
tanθ = tanα ⇒ θ = nπ + α
sinθ = 0 ⇒ θ = nπ
cosθ = 0 ⇒ θ = (2n+1)π/2
