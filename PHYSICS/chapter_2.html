⚡ electrostatic potential & capacitance

chapter 2 · one‑liner summaries — pure revision · dark mode
⚡ electric potential & diff
  • Electric potential: work done to bring +1C from infinity to that point.
  • Potential difference: work done to move +1C between two points.
  • Point charge: V = kq/r
  • Scalar quantity → directly add karte hain.
  • SI unit joule/coulomb = volt (V).
⚡ dipole potential
  • Axial point: V = kp/r² (short dipole).
  • Equatorial point: net potential = 0 (always).
p = dipole moment, r = distance
⚡ equipotential surface
  • Har point par potential same rehta hai.
  • Work done = 0 (charge move karne par).
  • Do surfaces kabhi intersect nahi karti.
  • Jahan surfaces close hoti hain, E strong hota hai.
⚡ E & V relation
  • E = – dV/dr (potential gradient ka negative).
  • Numerical trick: V = E·d (uniform field).
⚡ spherical shell
  • Inside shell: potential constant = surface value.
  • At surface: kQ/R
  • Outside (r): kQ/r
⚡ potential energy
  • System of charges: U = k q₁q₂/r (assembly work).
  • Dipole in uniform field: U = –pE cosθ
⚡ capacitance intro
  • Capacitor: device jo electrical energy store karta hai.
  • Capacitance (C): charge hold karne ki ability → Q = CV
  • Spherical capacitor: C = 4πϵ₀ R
  • Parallel plate: C = ϵ₀ A/d
⚡ dielectric & slab
  • Dielectric (K) dalne se capacitance K times badh jata hai.
  • Partial slab (thickness t): C = ϵ₀A / (d−t + t/K)
  • Energy stored: ½CV² = ½Q²/C
⚡ combination rules
  • Series: 1/Cnet = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + …
  • Parallel: Cnet = C₁ + C₂ + …
⚡ dielectrics — type
  • Polar (eg HCl) & non‑polar (eg CO₂).
  • Without external field → net dipole moment = 0.
  • Field lagane par non‑zero ho jata hai (polar / non‑polar both).
⚡ memory triggers
  • Ved trick: V = Ed (uniform field).
  • Equipotential → E perpendicular hota hai.
  • Dipole equatorial line: V = 0 always.
  • Capacitor energy = ½QV = ½CV² = ½Q²/C
