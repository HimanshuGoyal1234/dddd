Importance of Chemistry

Chemistry is the central science

Links physical sciences with life sciences and applied sciences.

Nature of Matter

Matter: Anything that has mass and occupies space.
States: Solid, Liquid, Gas

Classification of Nature of Matter

Pure substance: Elements (e.g. O₂, Na)
Pure substances: Compounds (e.g. H₂O, NaCl)
Mixtures: Homogeneous (e.g. salt in water)
Mixtures: Heterogeneous (e.g. sand in water)

Properties of Matter

Physical properties: Measured without changing composition (e.g. boiling point, density)
Chemical properties: Involve change in composition (e.g. rusting, burning)

Units of Measurement

Base SI units

Length: metre (m)
Mass: kilogram (kg)
Time: second (s)
Temperature: kelvin (K)
Amount of substance: mole (mol)

Derived units

Volume: m³
Density: kg/m³

Uncertainty in Measurement

Precision: How close values are to each other.
Accuracy: How close value is to true value.
Significant figures: Digits known with certainty plus one uncertain digit.

Laws of Chemical Combination

Law of Conservation of Mass: Mass can neither be created nor destroyed.
Law of Definite Proportions: A compound always contains elements in a fixed ratio.
Law of Multiple Proportions: When two elements form more than one compound, masses combine in ratios of small whole numbers.
Gay Lussac's Law: Gases combine in volumes which bear simple whole number ratio.
Avogadro's Law: Equal volumes of gases at same T and P contain equal number of molecules.

Dalton's Atomic Theory

  • All matter is made up of atoms.
  • Atoms of an element are identical.
  • Atoms combine in simple ratios to form compounds.

Mole Concept & Molar Mass

1 Mole = 6.022 × 10²³ particles (Avogadro Number)

Molar mass: Mass of 1 mole of a substance in grams.

Formulas

Molar mass = Mass of 1 mole of a substance in grams.
No. of moles = No. of particles / Avogadro number
Mass = moles × molar mass

Percentage Composition

Tells % by mass of each element in a compound.

Percentage of element = (Mass of element in 1 mol of compound / Molar Mass of compound) × 100

Empirical and Molecular Formula

Empirical formula: Simplest whole number ratio of atoms.

Steps to find Empirical Formula

  • % → grams
  • Convert grams → moles (divide by atomic mass)
  • Divide all moles by smallest number
  • Get Whole Numbers = Subscripts
Molecular formula = (Empirical Formula) × n
Where: n = Molecular mass / Empirical formula mass

Stoichiometry & Limiting Reagent

Stoichiometry: Deals with calculation of reactants/products using balanced chemical equations.
Limiting reagent: Reactant that gets consumed first and limits the amount of product.

Concentration Terms

Molarity (M): moles of solute / volume of solution (L)
Molality (m): moles of solute / mass of solvent (kg)
