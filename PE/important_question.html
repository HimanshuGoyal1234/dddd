1. What is a tournament? List various types of tournaments.
Tournament: A tournament is a competition involving multiple teams or individuals playing a series of games according to a set schedule to determine a champion.

Types of Tournaments:
  • Knockout (Elimination): Losing team is eliminated. (e.g., Wimbledon)
  • League (Round Robin): Every team plays every other team. (e.g., IPL)
  • Combination: Mix of knockout and league (e.g., group stage + knockouts).
  • Challenge: The champion plays against challengers. (e.g., Chess matches)

4. Merits and demerits of Single Knockout tournaments.
Merits: Minimal time required, fewer officials needed, exciting for spectators, low cost.
Demerits: Good teams may be eliminated early, only one chance, can be demoralizing, not a true test of all teams.
5. Merits and demerits of League (Round Robin) tournaments.
Merits: True champion emerges, every team plays all others, more matches = more practice, fair evaluation.
Demerits: Time-consuming, requires many officials and infrastructure, can be less exciting if winner already decided, higher cost.

1. What is a balanced diet? State its function.
Balanced diet: A diet that contains all essential nutrients (carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, water, fiber) in correct proportions to maintain health [citation:1].
Functions: Provides energy, supports growth and repair, regulates body processes, maintains immunity, prevents deficiency diseases.
2. Elaborate on the important nutrients/elements of a balanced diet.
Macronutrients: Carbs (energy), Proteins (growth), Fats (energy, insulation) [citation:2][citation:6].
Micronutrients: Vitamins (regulation), Minerals (bones, fluids) [citation:5].
Others: Water (hydration, transport), Fiber (digestion).
3. Elaborate: "Carbohydrates as energy-giving food." Mention sources and function.
Function: Primary energy source. 1g = 4 kcal. Spare protein, fuel brain and muscles [citation:2].
Sources: Grains (wheat, rice), fruits, vegetables, legumes, dairy [citation:1].
4. What are proteins? State their function.
Proteins: Polymers of amino acids. 9 essential amino acids must come from diet [citation:3].
Functions: Build and repair tissues, enzymes, hormones, antibodies, transport [citation:3][citation:6].
5. Write in brief about important minerals' requirements.
Major minerals: Calcium (bones), Sodium (fluid balance), Potassium (muscle), Phosphorus (energy).
Trace minerals: Iron (blood), Zinc (immunity), Iodine (thyroid) [citation:5].
6. Sports nutrition (fluid & meal intake pre-, during, post-competition) is necessary. Justify.
Pre-competition: Carb loading for energy stores. Light meal 3-4 hrs before.
During competition: Hydration (water/electrolytes), quick carbs to maintain blood glucose.
Post-competition: Protein for repair, carbs to refill glycogen, fluids to rehydrate.
7. Write a detailed note on the function of fats along with daily recommendation.
Functions: Concentrated energy (9 kcal/g), absorb vitamins (A, D, E, K), cell membranes, insulation, protect organs [citation:4][citation:6].
Recommendation: 20â€“35% of daily calories. Limit saturated fats (<10%), avoid trans fats. Prefer unsaturated (olive oil, nuts, fish) [citation:4].
8. Vitamins are of two types. Explain both.
Fat-soluble (A, D, E, K): Stored in body fat/liver, need fat for absorption, can be toxic in excess [citation:5].
Water-soluble (B-complex, C): Not stored, excreted in urine, need regular intake, sensitive to heat [citation:5].
9. Explain macro-nutrients and their role in our diet.
Macronutrients are needed in large amounts [citation:6]. Carbs: energy. Proteins: structure & repair. Fats: energy storage & protection.
10. What is dieting? Explain its pitfalls.
Dieting: Controlled eating for weight loss or health.
Pitfalls: Nutrient deficiencies, slow metabolism, muscle loss, eating disorders, yo-yo effect.
11. Discuss various food myths.
Myths: "Carbs are fattening" (Fact: whole grains are healthy), "Gluten-free is healthier" (Fact: not for non-celiac), "All fats are bad" (Fact: healthy fats needed) [citation:7].

1. Explain the procedure & benefits of Tadasana.
Procedure: Stand erect, feet together, arms by side. Raise arms overhead, interlock fingers, rise on toes, stretch whole body. Hold, then release [citation:8].

Procedure: Stand, raise arms, bend backward from waist.
