Chapter 8: Sports and Nutrition

1. Macronutrients for Athletes

Essential Macronutrients

Carbohydrates

  • Role: Primary energy source
  • Daily Need: 5-10g/kg body weight
  • Best Sources: Whole grains, fruits, vegetables

Proteins

  • Role: Muscle repair/growth
  • Daily Need: 1.2-2.0g/kg body weight
  • Best Sources: Lean meats, dairy, legumes

Ideal athlete's plate: 50% carbs, 25% protein, 25% vegetables

Timing Carbohydrates Protein Example

Post-Workout (within 30min) 1-1.2g/kg 0.3-0.4g/kg Chicken + rice + vegetables

2. Micronutrients and Hydration

Key Micronutrients for Athletes

Iron

Oxygen transport, prevents anemia

Sources: Red meat, spinach, lentils

Calcium

Bone health, muscle function

Sources: Dairy, leafy greens, almonds

Vitamin D

Bone health, immunity

Sources: Sunlight, fatty fish, eggs

Electrolytes

Hydration balance

Sources: Bananas, coconut water, sports drinks

Hydration Guidelines

Before Exercise

  • 500ml water 2-3 hours before
  • 250ml water 20-30 min before
  • Monitor urine color (pale yellow ideal)

During Exercise

  • 150-350ml every 15-20 min
  • Sports drink if >60 min intense activity
  • Weigh before/after to estimate sweat loss

3. Weight Management in Sports

Calculating Energy Needs

Step 1: Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Men: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age(y) + 5

Women: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age(y) - 161

Step 2: Activity Factor

  • Sedentary: ×1.2
  • Light activity: ×1.375
  • Moderate activity (3-5 days/wk): ×1.55
  • Very active (6-7 days/wk): ×1.725
  • Extra active (athlete): ×1.9

Step 3: Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor

Safe Weight Loss/Gain Strategies

Goal Caloric Adjustment Rate Key Tips
Weight Loss 300-500 kcal deficit/day 0.5-1kg/week High protein, resistance training
Weight Gain 300-500 kcal surplus/day 0.25-0.5kg/week Focus on lean mass, progressive overload

4. Sports-Specific Nutrition

Endurance vs Strength Athletes

Endurance Athletes (Marathon, Cycling)

  • Higher carb intake (6-10g/kg)
  • Moderate protein (1.2-1.6g/kg)
  • Focus on hydration/electrolytes
  • Carb-loading before events

Strength Athletes (Weightlifting, Sprinting)

  • Moderate carb intake (3-5g/kg)
  • Higher protein (1.6-2.2g/kg)
  • Creatine supplementation beneficial
  • Timed protein intake crucial

Competition Day Nutrition

  1. 3-4 Days Before: Carb-loading (7-10g/kg)
  2. Pre-Event (3-4hr): Familiar, high-carb meal
  3. 1hr Before: Small carb snack if needed
  4. During: 30-60g carbs/hr for >90min events
  5. Recovery: 1-1.2g/kg carbs + 0.3g/kg protein within 30min

Supplement Safety Considerations

  • Third-party tested products (NSF, Informed Sport)
  • Avoid proprietary blends with undisclosed amounts
  • Consult sports dietitian/doctor before use
  • More ≠ better - follow dosage guidelines
  • Check WADA prohibited list for competitive athletes
