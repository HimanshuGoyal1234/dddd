Flamingo (Poetry) – "Aunt Jennifer’s Tigers" by Adrienne Rich

SHORT SUMMARY

"Aunt Jennifer’s Tigers" explores themes of gender oppression and artistic expression. The poem describes Aunt Jennifer’s embroidery of majestic tigers, which symbolize strength and freedom—qualities she lacks in her own life. Trapped in a patriarchal marriage, Aunt Jennifer's hands tremble under the weight of her responsibilities. The poem contrasts her constrained existence with the bold and fearless tigers she creates.

Theme

Gender Oppression

The poem highlights the struggles of women in a patriarchal society, symbolized through Aunt Jennifer’s trembling hands.

Freedom vs. Confinement

The fearless tigers represent the freedom Aunt Jennifer desires but cannot attain.

Art as an Escape

Aunt Jennifer finds solace in her embroidery, creating a world where strength and independence exist.

Important Questions

What do Aunt Jennifer’s tigers symbolize?

They symbolize power, freedom, and confidence, which contrast with Aunt Jennifer’s oppressed life.

Why are Aunt Jennifer’s hands described as trembling?

Her hands tremble due to the weight of her marital constraints and societal pressures.

What does the poem say about gender roles?

It critiques traditional gender roles where women like Aunt Jennifer are burdened by societal expectations.

Key Quotes

"Aunt Jennifer’s tigers prance across a screen, bright topaz denizens of a world of green."

"The massive weight of Uncle’s wedding band Sits heavily upon Aunt Jennifer’s hand."

"When Aunt is dead, her terrified hands will lie still ringed with ordeals she was mastered by."

Literary Devices

Symbolism

The tigers symbolize freedom and power, while Aunt Jennifer represents oppressed women.

Metaphor

"The massive weight of Uncle’s wedding band" metaphorically represents the burdens of marriage.

Imagery

Vivid descriptions of the tigers and Aunt Jennifer’s struggles make the poem visually powerful.
