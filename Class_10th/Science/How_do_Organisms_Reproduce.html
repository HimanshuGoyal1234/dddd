How do Organisms Reproduce

Reproduction: The biological process by which new individual organisms are produced from their parents.

1. Types of Reproduction

Asexual vs Sexual Reproduction

Asexual Reproduction Sexual Reproduction
Single parent involved Two parents involved (male and female)
No formation of gametes Involves formation of gametes
Offspring are genetically identical (clones) Offspring show genetic variation
Examples: Binary fission in Amoeba, Budding in Hydra Examples: Human reproduction, Flowering plants

2. Asexual Reproduction Methods

Binary Fission

Amoeba:

Parent cell → Nucleus divides → Cytoplasm divides → Two daughter cells

Budding

Hydra:

Parent organism → Bud forms → Bud grows → Detaches to form new individual

3. Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants

Flower Structure

Male Part (Stamen): Anther + Filament

Female Part (Pistil): Stigma + Style + Ovary

Pollination to Fertilization

  1. Pollination (transfer of pollen to stigma)
  2. Pollen tube growth
  3. Double fertilization:
    • One sperm + egg → zygote
    • Other sperm + polar nuclei → endosperm
  4. Seed and fruit formation

4. Human Reproductive System

Male Reproductive System

  • Testes (produce sperm and testosterone)
  • Vas deferens (sperm duct)
  • Prostate gland and seminal vesicles (add fluids)
  • Penis (copulatory organ)

Female Reproductive System

  • Ovaries (produce eggs and hormones)
  • Fallopian tubes (site of fertilization)
  • Uterus (womb for embryo development)
  • Vagina (birth canal)

5. Reproductive Health

Contraceptive Methods

Type Examples How it Works
Barrier Condoms, Diaphragm Prevents sperm reaching egg
Hormonal Birth control pills Prevents ovulation
Surgical Vasectomy, Tubectomy Permanent blocking of tubes

6. Importance of Reproduction

  • Continuation of species
  • Introduction of variations for evolution
  • Maintenance of ecological balance
  • Replacement of aging population
