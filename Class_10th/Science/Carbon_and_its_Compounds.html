1. The Covalent Bond

Covalent Bond: Formed by sharing of electrons between atoms to achieve octet configuration (8 electrons in outer shell).
Example: Methane (CH4)

Carbon shares 4 electrons (tetravalency) with 4 hydrogen atoms.

Why Carbon Forms Covalent Bonds?

  • Small size → Strong nucleus-electron attraction
  • Tetravalency (4 valence electrons)
  • Catenation: Ability to form long chains

2. Allotropes of Carbon

Diamond

  • Tetrahedral structure
  • Hardest natural substance
  • Poor conductor

Graphite

  • Hexagonal layers
  • Soft and slippery
  • Good conductor

Fullerenes

  • Spherical molecules (C60)
  • Used in nanotechnology

3. Versatile Nature of Carbon

Property Explanation Example
Catenation Self-linking ability C-C-C chains in alkanes
Tetravalency Forms 4 bonds CH4, CCl4
Isomerism Same formula, different structure Butane vs Isobutane

4. Saturated vs Unsaturated Compounds

Saturated (Alkanes): C-C single bonds (CH3-CH3 Ethane)
Unsaturated: Alkenes (C=C), Alkynes (C≡C)

5. Homologous Series

Series General Formula Example
Alkanes CnH2n+2 CH4 (Methane)
Alkenes CnH2n C2H4 (Ethene)
Alkynes CnH2n-2 C2H2 (Ethyne)
