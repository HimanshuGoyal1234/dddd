Flamingo (Poetry) – "My Mother at Sixty-Six" by Kamala Das

SHORT SUMMARY

"My Mother at Sixty-Six" is a reflective poem by Kamala Das, capturing the emotions of a daughter observing her aging mother. The poet describes her mother's frail and pale appearance as she dozes off in the car. The imagery of young children and trees racing past contrasts with her mother’s aging, signifying the inevitable passage of time. Despite her sorrow, she tries to maintain a cheerful goodbye, hiding her fears of separation and loss.

Theme

Aging and Mortality

The poem highlights the natural process of aging and the inevitable reality of death.

Love and Fear of Separation

Expresses the poet’s deep love for her mother and her unspoken fear of losing her.

Childhood vs Old Age

The contrast between youthful energy and old age emphasizes the cycle of life.

Important Questions

What does the poet notice about her mother?

She notices that her mother looks pale and lifeless, resembling a corpse.

Why does the poet compare her mother to a ‘late winter’s moon’?

The comparison symbolizes her mother’s old age, weakness, and the approaching end of life.

What is the significance of ‘young trees sprinting’?

It contrasts the stillness of old age with the movement and energy of youth.

Why does the poet smile and say ‘See you soon, Amma’?

She hides her sorrow and fear of losing her mother by putting on a brave face.

Key Quotes

“I saw my mother, beside me, doze, open-mouthed, her face ashen like that of a corpse.”

“Trees sprinting, the merry children spilling out of their homes.”

“She looked but soon put that thought away, and looked out at Young Trees sprinting.”

“I smiled and smiled and smiled…”

Literary Devices

Simile

Comparing her mother’s face to a corpse and a ‘late winter’s moon’.

Personification

‘Young trees sprinting’ gives human-like qualities to trees.

Contrast

The contrast between her aging mother and the lively young children.

Repetition

‘Smiled and smiled and smiled’ emphasizes the poet’s effort to hide her pain.
