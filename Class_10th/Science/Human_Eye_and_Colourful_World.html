Human Eye and Colourful World

The human eye is one of the most valuable and sensitive sense organs that enables us to see the colourful world around us.

1. Structure of Human Eye

Main Parts of Human Eye

  • Cornea: Transparent front part
  • Iris: Colored diaphragm
  • Pupil: Opening that controls light entry
  • Lens: Focuses light on retina
  • Retina: Light-sensitive layer (contains rods and cones)
  • Optic Nerve: Carries signals to brain

2. Eye Defects and Corrections

Myopia (Near-sightedness)

Cause: Eyeball too long or lens too curved

Correction: Concave lens (diverging lens)

Power (P) = 1/focal length (f)

Hypermetropia (Far-sightedness)

Cause: Eyeball too short or lens too flat

Correction: Convex lens (converging lens)

3. Refraction of Light Through Prism

Dispersion of White Light

When white light passes through a prism, it splits into its constituent colors (VIBGYOR):

Violet deviates most, Red deviates least

This happens because different colors have different wavelengths and thus different refractive indices
